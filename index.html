§ 03Approach

Three things we refuse to do.

Most AI courses are selling a future. This one is about the next six classes — what the tools can actually do for your work, and what they cannot. No hype, no shortcuts that stop working the moment a new model ships.

01
Cohort size

Stay small.

Four to six people per track. No scaling, no cohort-of-five-hundred, no "community" that is really a Discord you will mute within a week. You will know everyone's name, and I will know what you are working on.

Max · 6 per cohort
02
What you build

Work on real things.

No toy exercises. Every prompt, every workflow, every output is tied to something from your actual life — an email you need to send, a post for your business, a decision you are trying to make. You leave with work, not worksheets.

Zero toy prompts
03
After the course

Yours to keep.

The prompt library, the scripts, the workflows — everything is yours after the course ends. No login that expires, no paywall you hit in month three. You should be able to do this without me, eventually.

All materials · Forever

  1. 01 Foundation

    [EN: Meet the tools — carefully.]

    [EN: We open ChatGPT, Claude, and the rest — the real ones, not the demos — and see where they’re brilliant and where they quietly go wrong. You leave with a working vocabulary.]

  2. 02 Craft

    [EN: Prompting, the careful way.]

    [EN: Role, context, task, format. We rewrite bad prompts together and keep the ones that hold up. No hacks. No gimmicks. Just the small structure that earns its place.]

    By the end [EN: Three prompts you’ll keep using.]
  3. 03 Your work

    [EN: The thing you actually do.]

    [EN: An email. A plan. A paper. A side project. We pick one piece of your real week and rebuild it with AI in the loop — then notice, honestly, what changed.]

    By the end [EN: One real task, done differently.]
  4. 04 Make

    [EN: Image. Sound. Motion.]

    [EN: Pick a medium. Make a real thing — a generated image, a short video, a cloned voice, a piece of music. Then we critique it, together, the way an editor would.]

    By the end [EN: One output you’re not embarrassed by.]
  5. 05 Restraint

    [EN: When to stop using it.]

    [EN: The other half of the skill. Which decisions is AI quietly making for you? Which ones do you need to take back? We audit a week of your own work — and your instincts get sharper.]

    By the end [EN: A sharper sense of when not to prompt.]
  6. 06 Show

    [EN: A final, real thing.]

    [EN: Something from your actual life — a portfolio piece, a school project, a job application, a side idea. You present it. We review it, carefully. You leave with one thing you’re ready to publish.]
